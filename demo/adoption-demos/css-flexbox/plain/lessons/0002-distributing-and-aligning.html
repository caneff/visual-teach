Step 2 — the workhorse skill

Distributing and aligning

The two properties you'll use every single day — one per axis.

Lesson 1 gave you a container and two axes. Now you put items exactly where you want them. There are really only two controls to learn, and they map cleanly onto the two axes you already know. Master them and the margin hacks retire for good.

Recall first Before reading on: a flex container's items lay out along the main axis, and the cross axis is perpendicular. If that feels shaky, take two minutes back in Lesson 1 — everything here is built on it.

The two controls, one per axis

Property Axis it controls In plain words
justify-content main axis Where items sit along the line, and how leftover space is shared.
align-items cross axis Where items sit across the line (e.g. vertically centered in a row).
gap between items Fixed space between items — no margins, no last-child exceptions.

justify-content takes six values worth knowing.1 The first three pile items together; the last three spread them out:

  • flex-start / flex-end / center — pack items at the start, end, or middle of the main axis.
  • space-between — first and last item hug the edges; equal gaps fill the middle.
  • space-around — equal space around each item (edges get a half-gap).
  • space-evenly — every gap, including the two edges, is identical.

align-items takes the cross-axis values: stretch (the default — items fill the cross axis), flex-start, flex-end, center, and baseline.1

The move you came for Perfect centering, finally. To center a box both ways, set its container to flex and center on both axes: 
.hero {
  display: flex;
  justify-content: center;  /* main axis  → horizontal */
  align-items: center;      /* cross axis → vertical   */
}
No margin: auto guessing, no position: absolute with transforms. Two lines.
