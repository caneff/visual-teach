Lesson 2 · The workhorse skill

Distributing and aligning

Position items along the main axis, align them on the cross axis — and finally center a box on purpose.

Why this lesson. This is the day the margin hacks die. Spacing a row of buttons evenly and centering a box — the two things that used to feel like luck — each become one declaration once you know which axis you're talking to. Built on the container & axis model from Lesson 1.

Two properties do almost all the work. One slides items along the main axis; the other aligns them on the cross axis. Learn which is which and the rest is muscle memory.

The two properties, by axis

justify-content distributes items along the main axis — the direction flex-direction chose. align-items aligns items on the cross axis. That's the entire idea; the values just say where. [MDN: aligning items]

.row {
  display: flex;
  justify-content: space-between;  /* along the main axis */
  align-items: center;            /* on the cross axis */
  gap: 1rem;                       /* minimum space between items */
}

justify-content — the six values

Imagine three boxes in a wide row. flex-start packs them at the start, flex-end at the end, center in the middle. The three space values distribute the leftover room:

  • space-between — first and last hug the edges; gaps equal between items.
  • space-around — equal space around each item, so edge gaps look half-size.
  • space-evenly — every gap identical, edges included.

align-items — centering, at last

On the cross axis, stretch (the default) makes items fill the container's cross size; flex-start / flex-end pin them to one edge; and center is the one you've been missing for vertical centering.

Your win this lesson justify-content: center + align-items: center = a box centered both ways, no margin: auto arithmetic, no position, no guessing. Two lines.

gap vs. the space-* values gap sets a minimum gutter between items and is the cleanest way to space a row — no margins on children, no last-child exceptions. justify-content: space-* distributes whatever room is left over after the items and gaps. They compose: use gap for the gutter, justify-content for where the whole group sits. [CSS-Tricks]

Common trap Trying to center vertically with justify-content on a default row. On flex-direction: row the main axis is horizontal, so justify-content moves items left/right. Vertical centering is the cross axis — that's align-items. Flip the direction to column and the two properties swap jobs. When a property "does nothing," you're usually talking to the wrong axis.
